⚠️ TRAINING DATA RESTRICTIONS & COMPLIANCE

## Prohibited Sources

## User-generated content without explicit, informed consent for AI training

## Confidential legal advice or privileged communications

## Court suppression orders or sealed documents

## Personal information not in the public domain

## Copyrighted materials without clear licensing for AI training

## Social media content behind privacy settings or without API permissions

## Data Processing Requirements

De-identification: Remove names, addresses, specific case details

Consent tracking: Maintain records of permissions for each data source

Version control: Track source document versions and updates

## Attribution requirements: Comply with Crown copyright and CC licensing terms

Retention limits: Follow data retention schedules per source agreements

## Legal Review Process

Pre-training review: Legal team approval for each new data source

Content validation: Verify accuracy and currency of legal information

Bias testing: Review for discriminatory or outdated legal concepts

Output testing: Validate AI responses against current legal standards

Source the primary legislation texts (official government websites or AustLII) for each item above — train on statutes, regulations, official guidance, and government fact sheets rather than thirdparty summaries.

Include agency guidance, complaint forms, and published decision summaries from the state antidiscrimination agencies, tribunals, and courts.

### For each jurisdiction, include:

## Key legislation (Acts & Regulations)

## Agency guidance & forms

## Police reporting pages and victim support resources

## Translation/ multicultural materials

## State privacy and surveillance laws

Build jurisdictional metadata: tag every source by jurisdiction, topic, and date so RAG picks the correct, current law for the user’s location.

Maintain an “expiry/refresh” schedule: laws change — run quarterly or eventdriven legal refreshes and keep version history.

Legal vetting: every legal text included for training must be cleared by counsel (copyright/licence, relevance, accuracy).

### For the avatar’s legal-sounding outputs:

Clearly label outputs as “information only — not legal advice.”

Include citations (Act, section, and date) and links to the authoritative source where possible.

Implement a humanintheloop for any response that could meaningfully influence legal decisions (advice on litigation, reporting strategies).

### Train the AI only on publicly available, authoritative legal sources from:

The Commonwealth (Cth) – all relevant Acts, Regulations, Guidelines, and public decisions.

All 8 Australian jurisdictions – NSW, VIC, QLD, SA, WA, TAS, NT, ACT.

No user data, no confidential content, no internal documents, no third-party commentary unless explicitly licensed.

📌 3. What to Train On (Specific Content Types)

### ✅ Include in training corpus:

## Full text of Acts, Regulations, Schedules (not summaries).

## Official guidance documents (fact sheets, FAQs, complaint instructions).

## Public decision summaries from tribunals/courts (e.g., NCAT, VCAT, AAT).

Complaint forms and reporting flow diagrams (e.g., how to report racism to eSafety or Anti-Discrimination NSW).

Multilingual resources (translated materials from Home Affairs, settlement services, Legal Aid).

### ❌ Never train on:

## User messages, chat logs, or case stories (even anonymised).

## Internal legal opinions, unapproved drafts, or private memos.

Third-party blogs, news articles, or opinion pieces (unless they’re quoting official texts).

## Court suppression orders, sealed documents, or non-public records.

## A. Data Pipeline Design

Store all source material in a version-controlled knowledge base (e.g., Pinecone, Milvus, Elasticsearch).

### Tag every document with:

jurisdiction: NSW, VIC, Cth, etc.

topic: discrimination, DV, privacy, online safety, workplace, evidence, child protection, etc.

source_type: Act, Guideline, Form, Decision, Report

last_updated: date of revision (auto-refresh quarterly)

license_status: "Public domain", "CC BY", "Government copyright"

## B. Model Training Mode

## Use Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), not full fine-tuning.

## Never embed raw statutes into model weights.

At inference time: retrieve relevant laws → present them with citations → add disclaimer.

## C. Output Safeguards

### Every AI response must include:

text

⚠️ This information is for general awareness only and does not constitute legal advice.  For personalized legal help, contact a registered lawyer or free service like:  - Legal Aid NSW: 1300 888 432  - Anti-Discrimination NSW: 131 444  - eSafety Commissioner: 1300 476 262

Again it is important we understand We are building an AI that knows every anti-discrimination, domestic violence, privacy, online safety, and workplace law in Australia — federally and across all states.

We will train it only on official, publicly available legal texts and guidance from government websites.

## No user data. No summaries. No blogs. Only primary sources.

## We will use RAG so we never hallucinate laws.

## Every answer will be backed by a citation and include a strong disclaimer.
